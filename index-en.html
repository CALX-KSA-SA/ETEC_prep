Why is Accreditation Important?

School Evaluation

School evaluation is a systematic process conducted by the Education and Training Evaluation Commission (ETEC) to measure the extent to which private and international schools meet approved national standards.

Accreditation

Accreditation is the official certification granted by the Commission to a school after successfully passing the evaluation process, proving its commitment to national education quality standards.

Official Reference

For more information, visit the official website: etec.gov.sa

Assessment Stages

1

Registration

Registering the school in the evaluation system and scheduling the visit.

2

Self-Study (SER)

Preparing a comprehensive report on school performance against ETEC standards.

3

Field Visit

Site visit by external evaluation team for 2-4 days.

4

Final Report

Issuance of comprehensive evaluation report and final classification.

Important Alert

Weaknesses & Challenges

Why do international schools face difficulties?

Lack of Documentation

Absence of tangible evidence and documented records proving exact compliance with standards.

Weak Self-Evaluation

Lack of internal mechanisms to measure performance honestly and transparently, leading to surprises during visits.

Misaligned Curriculum

International curricula not integrated with national identity and local mandatory requirements.

Accreditation Challenges

Indicators suggest many schools face challenges meeting standards in initial visits due to lack of accurate documentation.
